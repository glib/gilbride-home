## In the beginning

As I turned fourty this month, I found myself thinking back to the internet I
grew up with; the internet that only started to really exist around my tenth
birthday (at least in any way that I can remember) but hit me like a lightning
bolt as soon as it showed up in my life. It was only something that existed at a
university, and then later, on a single computer at school and three computers
at the local library. While every other computer in the 'computer lab' at
elementary school (_do these still exist?_) was an apple II loaded with maven
teaches typing (and oregon trail if you finished early), this one in the corner
had a new high resolution screen, windows 95 and its own dedicated modem and
phone line. This was the first place where we got to 'make a website' - which
meant hand-coding some HTML in notepad, saving it off on a floppy, taking turns
on the new computer which has a browser to display your result.

I remember absolutely loving this experience, and when we got the internet at
home a year or so later on our 33.6 baud modem, I started down the journey of
making things and looking at how other people were making their things.

## We have to go back (Jack)

> "Be the change you want to see in the world"

A quote usually attributed to Gandhi but more likely crafted by some unknown
author years later. Nonetheless it feels authentic and true. Like so many others
who work in tech (and many who do not), I find myself increasingly frustrated
with the state of the internet. Social media is so bad that simply removing an
app from my phone without even doing anything as drastic as deleting or even
suspending an account is enough to dramatically improve my mood and mental
health (bye Instragram!).

And this is more text.
